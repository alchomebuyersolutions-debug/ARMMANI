Term 1 of 5

PoC — Point of Control

The price level with the HIGHEST volume traded

The PoC represents where the most buying and selling happened — the center of the battlefield. Price is magnetically attracted to this level.

Think of PoC as "fair value" — it acts as a magnet. Strong PoCs from previous sessions become key support/resistance levels.

Term 2 of 5

VaH — Value Area High

The upper boundary of the 70-80% volume zone

VaH marks the top of the value area. Price ABOVE VaH is in premium territory — above where most participants agreed on value.

Price breaking above VaH = strong bullish momentum. Price rejecting at VaH from below = short opportunity. Think of VaH as the "ceiling" of fair value.

Term 3 of 5

VaL — Value Area Low

The lower boundary of the 70-80% volume zone

VaL is the floor of the value area. Price BELOW VaL is at a discount — below where most participants agreed on fair value.

Price bouncing off VaL = buyers defending (long entry). Price crashing through VaL = strong bearish shift. VaL is your "floor" of the battlefield.

Term 4 of 5

HVN — High Volume Node

Fortified positions where big players committed capital

HVNs are thick clusters in the volume profile. They act as strong support/resistance because traders will defend their positions here.

Lorenzo's "magic touch" — when price returns to an HVN, it SLOWS DOWN, consolidates, or reverses. Trade the bounce, not the break.

Term 5 of 5

LVN — Low Volume Node

No man's land — price accelerates through

LVNs are gaps in the volume profile. Nobody is defending these levels, so price moves FAST through them.

Don't place entries in the middle of an LVN — you'll get run over. Wait for price to reach the HVN on the other side. LVNs are transit zones, not battle zones.
